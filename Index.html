Anthony Edward Stark — engineer, inventor, and founding member of the Avengers — built his first suit of armor in a cave with only scraps and a box of spare parts. What started as an escape mechanism became the most sophisticated combat system ever devised.

Powered by the Arc Reactor, the Iron Man suit is not merely a weapon. It is an extension of Stark himself: adaptive, intelligent, and relentlessly evolving. Each iteration represents a new response to a new threat — refined through failure, sharpened by necessity.

From the crude Mark I to the nanotech-infused Mark L, the Iron Man legacy is one of transformation. Not just of metal — but of a man who chose, again and again, to be more than what the world asked of him.
